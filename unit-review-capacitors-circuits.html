1.

Two capacitors of equal capacitance C are first charged to a potential difference V and then removed from the battery and connected to each other (positive plate to positive plate). The final energy of the system is:

A $$\frac{1}{4}CV^2$$
B $$\frac{1}{2}CV^2$$
C $$CV^2$$
D $$2CV^2$$
E $$4CV^2$$
Hint: Total charge = 2CV, total capacitance = 2C. Find $$V_{final}$$ then $$U = \frac{1}{2}(2C)V_{final}^2$$

2.

A 20 μF capacitor is fully charged by a 100 V battery. The total energy delivered by the battery to charge the capacitor is:

A 0.02 J
B 0.05 J
C 0.1 J
D 0.2 J
E 0.4 J
Hint: Energy delivered by battery = $$QV = CV^2$$. Energy stored = $$\frac{1}{2}CV^2$$. Battery delivers TWICE the stored energy.

3.

A parallel-plate capacitor with plate area A and separation d is fully charged to voltage V and then disconnected from the battery. If the plate separation is then reduced to d/2, which of the following is true?

A The voltage doubles and the charge halves
B Both voltage and charge are unchanged
C The voltage halves and the charge is unchanged
D The voltage is unchanged and the charge doubles
E The voltage halves and the charge doubles
Hint: Disconnected $$\Rightarrow$$ q is constant. $$C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$. What happens to C? Then use $$V = \frac{q}{C}$$.

4.

A circuit contains a battery of EMF ε and three identical resistors R. Two resistors are in parallel, and that combination is in series with the third. The current through the battery is:

A $$\frac{\varepsilon}{3R}$$
B $$\frac{2\varepsilon}{3R}$$
C $$\frac{\varepsilon}{2R}$$
D $$\frac{\varepsilon}{R}$$
E $$\frac{3\varepsilon}{2R}$$
Hint: $$R_{parallel} = \frac{R}{2}$$. Total $$R = R + \frac{R}{2} = \frac{3R}{2}$$. Use Ohm's law.

5.

In an RC circuit, a capacitor is being charged. At time t = τ (one time constant), the charge on the capacitor is approximately:

A 37% of maximum
B 50% of maximum
C 63% of maximum
D 86% of maximum
E 95% of maximum
Hint: $$q(\tau) = Q_{max}(1 - e^{-1})$$. Evaluate numerically.

6.

A parallel-plate capacitor with air gap has capacitance C. A dielectric slab with κ = 3 that fills the entire gap is inserted while the battery remains connected. Which quantities change and by what factor?

A Charge triples, voltage unchanged, energy triples
B Charge triples, voltage triples, energy remains same
C Charge unchanged, voltage triples, energy triples
D Charge remains same, voltage drops by 3, energy drops by 3
E Charge and voltage both triple, energy increases by 9
Hint: Battery connected $$\Rightarrow$$ V is fixed. $$C \rightarrow \kappa C$$. Track q and U.

7.

In a circuit with a 12V battery and internal resistance 2Ω connected to an external resistance R, the power dissipated in R is maximized when:

A R = 0
B R = 1Ω
C R = 2Ω
D R = 4Ω
E R = ∞
Hint: Maximum power transfer theorem. When is $$P_R = I^2 R$$ maximized?

8.

Two capacitors C1 = 3 μF and C2 = 6 μF are connected in series to a 12 V battery. The voltage across C1 is:

A 2V
B 4V
C 6V
D 8V
E 12V
Hint: Series $$\Rightarrow$$ same charge. Find $$C_{eq}$$ first, then q, then $$V_1 = \frac{q}{C_1}$$.

9.

An RC circuit has R = 5 MΩ and C = 2 μF. The time constant is:

A 2.5 s
B 5 s

A parallel-plate capacitor stores energy U. If the plate area is doubled AND the separation is halved, the new energy stored (with the same battery connected) is:

B U

Answer Key

Multiple Choice Answers

Question Answer Key Reasoning
1 C — $$CV^2$$ Both caps hold $$CV$$, so total $$Q = 2CV$$. Capacitances in parallel: $$C_{total} = 2C$$. $$V_{final} = \frac{Q}{2C} = V$$. $$U = \frac{1}{2}(2C)V^2 = CV^2$$.
2 D — 0.2 J $$E_{delivered} = CV^2 = (20 \times 10^{-6})(100^2) = 0.20 \text{ J}$$. Note: stored energy $$= \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = 0.10 \text{ J}$$. Battery delivers twice the stored energy.
3 C — Voltage halves, charge unchanged Disconnected $$\Rightarrow$$ $$q$$ constant. $$d \to d/2 \Rightarrow C$$ doubles. $$V = q/C \Rightarrow V$$ halves.
4 B — $$\frac{2\varepsilon}{3R}$$ $$R_{||} = R/2$$. $$R_{total} = R + R/2 = 3R/2$$. $$I = \varepsilon/(3R/2) = 2\varepsilon/(3R)$$.
5 C — 63% $$q(\tau) = Q_{max}(1 - e^{-1}) = Q_{max}(1 - 0.368) = 0.632 Q_{max} \approx 63\%$$.
6 A — Charge triples, V unchanged, energy triples Battery connected $$\Rightarrow$$ $$V$$ fixed. $$C \to \kappa C = 3C$$. $$q = CV \to 3CV$$. $$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 \to 3(\frac{1}{2}CV^2)$$.
7 C — $$R = 2\Omega$$ Maximum power transfer theorem: $$P_R$$ is maximized when $$R = r = 2\Omega$$.
8 D — 8V $$C_{eq} = \frac{C_1 C_2}{C_1 + C_2} = \frac{18}{9} = 2 \text{ μF}$$. $$q = 2(12) = 24 \text{ μC}$$. $$V_1 = q/C_1 = 24/3 = 8 \text{ V}$$.
9 C — 10 s $$\tau = RC = (5 \times 10^6)(2 \times 10^{-6}) = 10 \text{ s}$$.
10 D — $$4U$$ $$C_{new} = \frac{\varepsilon_0(2A)}{d/2} = 4 \varepsilon_0 A / d = 4C_0$$. Battery connected $$\Rightarrow$$ same $$V$$. $$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 \Rightarrow U_{new} = 4U$$.

Free Response Solutions

Problem 1: Capacitor Circuit Analysis

(a) Equivalent Capacitance:
$$C_{23} = C_2 + C_3 = 4 + 6 = 10 \text{ μF}$$
$$C_{eq} = \frac{C_1 \cdot C_{23}}{C_1 + C_{23}} = \frac{(2)(10)}{2 + 10} = \frac{20}{12} = \frac{5}{3} \text{ μF} \approx 1.67 \text{ μF}$$
(b) Charge on C₁:
Since $$C_1$$ is in series with the $$C_{23}$$ combination, they hold the same charge.
$$q = C_{eq} V = \frac{5}{3}(24) = 40 \text{ μC}$$
(c) Voltage Across C₂:
$$V_{23} = \frac{q}{C_{23}} = \frac{40}{10} = 4.0 \text{ V}$$
Since $$C_2$$ and $$C_3$$ are in parallel, they both see the same voltage: $$V_2 = V_3 = 4.0 \text{ V}$$
(d) Energy Stored in Each Capacitor:
$$U_1 = \frac{1}{2}\frac{q^2}{C_1} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{(40 \times 10^{-6})^2}{2 \times 10^{-6}} = 400 \text{ μJ}$$
$$U_2 = \frac{1}{2}C_2 V_{23}^2 = \frac{1}{2}(4 \times 10^{-6})(4^2) = 32 \text{ μJ}$$
$$U_3 = \frac{1}{2}C_3 V_{23}^2 = \frac{1}{2}(6 \times 10^{-6})(4^2) = 48 \text{ μJ}$$
$$U_{total} = 400 + 32 + 48 = 480 \text{ μJ}$$
(e) With Dielectric and Battery Disconnected:
Total charge is conserved: $$q = 40 \text{ μC}$$ (constant).
New capacitance of $$C_1$$: $$C_1' = \kappa C_1 = 2(2) = 4 \text{ μF}$$
$$V_1' = \frac{q}{C_1'} = \frac{40}{4} = 10 \text{ V}$$
$$V_{23}' = \frac{q}{C_{23}} = \frac{40}{10} = 4 \text{ V}$$
New total voltage: $$V_{total}' = 10 + 4 = 14 \text{ V}$$ (increased from 24V)
This seems odd—let me reconsider. Actually, when the battery is disconnected first, the total charge on the circuit decreases. Let me recalculate: Initially, $$q = 40 \text{ μC}$$ on $$C_1$$ and the $$C_{23}$$ combo. After disconnect, this charge is locked in. When we insert the dielectric, $$C_1$$ increases, so $$V_1$$ drops and $$V_2, V_3$$ must increase to conserve total voltage drop (but there's no battery, so this is odd). Actually, the issue is that we're inserting a dielectric into a portion of the circuit with fixed charge. The voltages redistribute. Final answer: $$V_1' = 10 \text{ V}$$, $$V_2' = V_3' = 4 \text{ V}$$.

Problem 2: RC Charging Circuit

(a) Time Constant:
$$\tau = RC = (50 \times 10^3)(100 \times 10^{-6}) = 5.0 \text{ s}$$
(b) Expressions for i(t), V_R(t), V_C(t), and q(t):
$$i(t) = \frac{\varepsilon}{R}e^{-t/\tau} = \frac{20}{50 \times 10^3}e^{-t/5} = 0.40 \text{ mA} \cdot e^{-t/5}$$
$$V_R(t) = \varepsilon e^{-t/\tau} = 20 e^{-t/5} \text{ V}$$
$$V_C(t) = \varepsilon(1 - e^{-t/\tau}) = 20(1 - e^{-t/5}) \text{ V}$$
$$q(t) = C\varepsilon(1 - e^{-t/\tau}) = 2000(1 - e^{-t/5}) \text{ μC}$$
(c) Values at Key Times:
Time i (mA) V_R (V) V_C (V) q (μC)
t = 0 0.40 20.0 0.0 0
t = τ = 5 s 0.147 7.36 12.64 1264
t = 2τ = 10 s 0.054 2.71 17.29 1729
t → ∞ 0 0.0 20.0 2000
(d) When V_C = V_R:
$$\varepsilon(1 - e^{-t/\tau}) = \varepsilon e^{-t/\tau}$$
$$1 = 2e^{-t/\tau}$$
$$e^{-t/\tau} = 0.5$$
$$t = \tau \ln(2) = 5 \ln(2) = 3.47 \text{ s}$$
Current at this moment: $$i = 0.40(0.5) = 0.20 \text{ mA}$$
(e) Discharge After Battery Removal:
Initial charge on capacitor: $$Q_0 = 2000 \text{ μC}$$
$$q(t) = Q_0 e^{-t/\tau} = 2000 e^{-t/5} \text{ μC}$$
$$i(t) = -\frac{Q_0}{\tau}e^{-t/\tau} = -0.40 e^{-t/5} \text{ mA}$$ (negative: discharging)
For $$q = 0.1 Q_0$$:
$$0.1 = e^{-t/5}$$
$$t = -5 \ln(0.1) = 5 \ln(10) = 11.5 \text{ s}$$

Problem 3: Parallel Plate Capacitor with Conducting Slab

(a) Initial Capacitance and Field:
$$C_0 = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
$$E_0 = \frac{V_0}{d}$$
(b) Capacitance with Slab:
The conducting slab is equipotential and has E = 0 inside. The effective separation is $$d - t$$.
$$C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d - t}$$
This is larger than $$C_0$$ by a factor of $$\frac{d}{d-t}$$.
(c) Charge with Slab:
$$q = CV_0 = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A V_0}{d - t}$$
(d) Electric Field in Air Gaps:
The field is uniform in the air gaps and zero inside the slab:
$$E = \frac{V_0}{d - t}$$
(Same in both gaps above and below the slab.)
(e) After Battery Disconnect and Slab Removal:
With slab, charge: $$q = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A V_0}{d - t}$$ (locked in when battery disconnects)
After slab removal: $$C_{removed} = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
New voltage: $$V_{new} = \frac{q}{C_{removed}} = \frac{V_0 d}{d - t} > V_0$$
Initial energy (with slab): $$U_i = \frac{1}{2}qV_0$$
Final energy (slab removed): $$U_f = \frac{1}{2}q V_{new} = \frac{1}{2}q \frac{V_0 d}{d-t} > U_i$$
Energy increased by factor of $$\frac{d}{d-t}$$. The extra energy came from the work you did pulling the slab out against the attractive electric force.

Problem 4: Coaxial Cable Capacitor

(a) Electric Field:
Using Gauss's law with a cylindrical surface of radius $$r$$ (where $$a < r < b$$) and length $$L$$:
$$\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{enc}}{\kappa \varepsilon_0}$$
$$E(2\pi r L) = \frac{q}{\kappa \varepsilon_0}$$
$$E = \frac{q}{2\pi \kappa \varepsilon_0 r L}$$
(b) Potential Difference:
$$V = \int_a^b E \, dr = \int_a^b \frac{q}{2\pi \kappa \varepsilon_0 L} \frac{dr}{r} = \frac{q}{2\pi \kappa \varepsilon_0 L} \ln\left(\frac{b}{a}\right)$$
(c) Capacitance:
$$C = \frac{q}{V} = \frac{2\pi \kappa \varepsilon_0 L}{\ln(b/a)}$$
(d) Energy per Unit Length:
Energy density: $$U = \frac{1}{2}(C/L)V_0^2 = \frac{\pi \kappa \varepsilon_0 V_0^2}{\ln(b/a)}$$
(This is energy per unit length.)
(e) Numerical Calculation:
Given: κ = 2.3, a = 0.5 mm = 0.5 × 10⁻³ m, b = 3.0 mm = 3.0 × 10⁻³ m, L = 50 m
$$\ln(b/a) = \ln(3.0/0.5) = \ln(6) = 1.792$$
$$C = \frac{2\pi (2.3)(8.854 \times 10^{-12})(50)}{1.792}$$
$$C = \frac{2\pi (2.3)(8.854 \times 10^{-12})(50)}{1.792} = \frac{6.37 \times 10^{-9}}{1.792} = 3.55 \text{ nF}$$

Problem 5: Multi-loop Circuit with RC

(a) Steady State (Switch Closed for Long Time):
After a long time, the capacitor is fully charged and acts as an open circuit. No current flows through C.
Total resistance: $$R_{total} = r + R_1 + R_2 = 1 + 4 + 12 = 17 \text{ Ω}$$
Total current: $$I = \frac{\varepsilon}{R_{total}} = \frac{30}{17} = 1.765 \text{ A}$$
Voltage across $$R_2$$ (and capacitor): $$V_C = I \cdot R_2 = 1.765 \times 12 = 21.18 \text{ V}$$
Current through $$R_2$$: 1.765 A (same as total)
Current through C: 0 A (fully charged)
(b) At Switch Opening:
Immediately after the switch opens, the capacitor voltage cannot change instantaneously.
$$V_C = 21.18 \text{ V}$$ (unchanged at $$t = 0^+$$)
Current through $$R_2$$: $$I = \frac{V_C}{R_2} = \frac{21.18}{12} = 1.765 \text{ A}$$
Direction: From positive plate of C through $$R_2$$ to negative plate (discharging).
(c) Voltage Across Capacitor During Discharge:
$$V_C(t) = 21.18 \, e^{-t/\tau}$$ where $$\tau = R_2 C$$
(d) Time Constant for Discharge:
$$\tau = R_2 C = (12)(100 \times 10^{-6}) = 1.2 \times 10^{-3} \text{ s} = 1.2 \text{ ms}$$
(e) Energy Dissipated in R₂:
Initial energy in C: $$U_i = \frac{1}{2}C V_C^2 = \frac{1}{2}(100 \times 10^{-6})(21.18)^2 = 22.4 \text{ mJ}$$
Energy dissipated: $$E_{dissipated} = \int_0^{\infty} I^2 R_2 \, dt = \int_0^{\infty} \left(\frac{V_C^2}{R_2}\right) e^{-2t/\tau} \, dt$$
$$= \frac{V_C^2}{R_2} \cdot \frac{\tau}{2} = \frac{(21.18)^2}{12} \cdot \frac{1.2 \times 10^{-3}}{2} = 22.4 \text{ mJ}$$
This equals the initial energy stored in C, confirming energy conservation.
